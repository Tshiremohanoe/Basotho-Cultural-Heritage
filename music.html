Basotho Music & Dance

Music and dance are an essential part of Basotho cultural expressions,rituals along side with storytelling.

Traditional Basotho Dance

Mohobelo

A men's dance charecterized by stamping and rhythmic movements,often performed in groups.Lately it is perfomed during the cultural activities and also during King Letsie 3rd's birthday ceremonies.

Litolobonya

A traditional women's dance, often perfomed at social gatherings and ceremonies.

Mokorotlo

This dance was named after iconic Basotho hat and only men and boys participate in it.It symbolizes hard work,respect and community unity.

Mokhibo

This is women's traditional dance perfomed on the knees,determining grace and femine virtues.It is also perfomed at initiation ceremonies and festive celebrations.

Litema

This is Basotho women's dance that they perform during the story telling and cultural life experiences.

Selete

This is Basotho traditional dance,characterized by rhythmic movements that celebrate culture and heritage.

Lebetlela Dance

This is whereby Basotho men as they incoporate through rhythmic footwork and gestures and moral teachings.

Thomo Dance

It is perfomed by Basotho youth during the celebrations showcasing their cultural dances to the nations and also as a lesson to tourists so that they can have ultimate experience at their stay at Lesotho.

Ntlamo

It is perfomed by Basotho men and boys,as they lift thier legs upside downs and as they are also singing during their performance.It often perfomed at the King Bereng Seeiso's birthday celebrations and other cultural ceremonies.

Khotso

It Basotho mes's dance that was perfomed by powerful rythmic movements, symbolizing strength and leadership.

Setipe Dance

It is perfomed by Basotho women highlighting expresssive movements.This dance is perfomed with colourful blankets at social events.

Seshoe

This Basotho dance, that was performed during the weddings and coronations highlighting heritage and culture by everyone as itbis general dance.

Sefaha Dance

This dance involves clay beards and traditional skirts performed during rites,invoking identity and beauty.It is perfomed by young girls and ladies.

Seleteng sa Bohlanka

This is community dance,celebrating the achievements in their society and unity among themselves as community members.

Likhobong

This is Basotho male dance showcasing agility and endurance and it is perfomed during cultural festives.

Maqekha

A traditional female dance involving drumming and ritualized movements symbolyzing maturity and caltural values.

Lebollo

A traditional initiation dance for young men or women,marking rites of passage and commual incorporation into adulthood.

Seana Marena

Commemorates king Moshoeshoe 1,the founder of Basotho nation,involving mimic warrier actions and celebrating historical resilience,unity and ancestral tribute.

Setolotolo

Traditional dance for women during thr weddings and harvest celebrations to honor fetility and female roles.

Tholoana

This dance emphasizes energetic footwork,embodies communal joy and festivity.

Bolo Sela

It is performed as to celebrate weddings or joyous events,conveys love,union and social togetherness.

Mokorotlo-Ts'epo

This dance indicates highest praises to chiefs or community heros as to honor them or showing appreciation to what they have done for community,so, they would perfom this dance.

Basotho Musical Instruments

Lesiba

A mouth-resonated string instrument producing unique sounds,traditionally used for herding calls.

Thomo

A traditional plucked instrument often played by women for storytelling and relaxation.

Mamokhorong

It is also known as "Sekhankula".This musical instrument is played by Basotho men,it is designed using string,stick and tin container for sweet sound release.

Tsolo

This is traditional drum made up by wood and animal skins, it is used in ceremonies,dances and social gatherings to add vibe in te celebrations.

Lesiba

A stringed-wind instrument consisting of a single string streched over a woodenframe with a flattened quill sounded by the player's breath to create haunting melodic tones.

Moropa

A tin covered by the ruber that makes a sweet sounds also it has a wire that consists the leads of the glass bottles called "mapanaki"<.

Setolo-tolo

Mouth bow producing harmonic tones,used historically for communucation or accompaniment during storytelling.

Lekolulo

A flute like aerophone traditionally crafted from reed or wood, it creates sweet melodic lines in folk music.

Lebetlela

A stringed instrument that adds depth and resonate to musical esembles through plucked or resonated notes.

Lekope

An endangered string instrument derived from bow-string phototypes, instrumental in senerates and cultural storytelling.

Mokhope

A traditional chordphone with melodic and rhythmic functions, often handmade from local materials.

Sekebeku

An idiophone producing sound through striking,it is used for rhythm and ceremonial occations.

Lekoko

A drum made up of hardened cowhide rolled into into a cylinder, played with sticks, often restricted to ritual performances.

Tempe

Traditional drum family made from hallowed-out logs covered with animal skin, used in dance and social ceremonies.

Famo Drum

A drum associated with Basotho Famo music, sometimes used to accent dance or song rhythms.

Maqekha Drum

A small ceremonial drum used in girl's initiation rites and sacred dances.

Mokorotlo Drum

Accompanie the traditional mokorotlo dance with slow swinging beats.

Plastic Guitar

This is Basotho musical instrument made up using plastic bottle,woodstone and strings called "lesika".It also produces sweet melodic sounds.

Lefotho

Musical bow ,less common but used for solo entertainment.

Sehonghong

A children's bow instruments that produces light along with playful tones.

Lipitse

Small hand-held drums for festive gatherings.

Leqhubu

A drum made from animal skin and wood producing deep sounds.

Likopelo

Calabash percussion instruments struck to keep time.
